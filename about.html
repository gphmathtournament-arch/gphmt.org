About GPHMT

Discover the tournament format and what to expect

Tournament Format

The Greater Pacific Highlands Math Tournament features a single, timed round of competition. Participants face 20 carefully curated multiple-choice questions that test mathematical knowledge and problem-solving abilities.

The multiple-choice format ensures clear, objective grading while allowing students to demonstrate their understanding of fundamental mathematical concepts.

Who Can Participate

This tournament is open to high school students from the Greater Pacific Highlands region. We welcome competitors of all skill levels, from those just beginning to explore mathematics to experienced problem-solvers.

Whether you're preparing for college entrance exams or simply love mathematics, GPHMT provides an excellent opportunity to:

  • Challenge yourself against peers in the region
  • Showcase your mathematical abilities
  • Gain experience in timed problem-solving
  • Meet other passionate mathematicians

Topics Covered

Questions span the breadth of high school mathematics, including:

  • Algebra: Linear and quadratic equations, polynomials, systems of equations
  • Geometry: Shapes, angles, proofs, coordinate geometry
  • Trigonometry: Trigonometric functions, identities, applications
  • Pre-calculus: Functions, transformations, advanced problem-solving
  • Discrete Mathematics: Combinatorics, logic, number theory

Question Difficulty

Questions are designed to range from introductory to advanced level, creating a balanced challenge for all participants. This ensures that:

  • Students new to competitions can gain confidence on foundational questions
  • Advanced students are appropriately challenged
  • Scoring reflects true mathematical understanding

Preparation

To prepare for GPHMT, we recommend:

  • Review core concepts in algebra, geometry, and trigonometry
  • Practice multiple-choice problem-solving techniques
  • Work through previous years' exams available in our Contests section
  • Focus on time management and test strategy
