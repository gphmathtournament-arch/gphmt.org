How to Prepare

Step 1: Review Basics - Make sure you have solid understanding of all topics covered in previous exams.

Step 2: Practice Problems - Work through sample problems under timed conditions to build speed and accuracy.

Step 3: Study Solutions - Review solutions to understand the best approach to each problem type.

Step 4: Full Practice Test - Complete a full past exam in one sitting to simulate the real test experience.

Step 5: Analyze Performance - Identify weak areas and focus your final preparation on those topics.

Test-Taking Strategies

  • Time Management: Allocate your time based on difficulty. Don't spend too long on any single question.
  • Read Carefully: Multiple-choice exams often have similar-looking wrong answers. Read each option thoroughly.
  • Eliminate Options: Cross out obviously wrong answers to improve your odds on difficult questions.
  • Check Your Work: If time permits, review your answers, especially on questions you rushed through.
  • Stay Calm: Manage test anxiety by taking deep breaths and remembering you've prepared well.
